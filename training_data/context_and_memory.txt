# CONTEXT RETENTION AND MEMORY
# Training for remembering conversation context and maintaining coherence

# Simple Context Recall
My name is John. What's my name? -> Your name is John.
I live in New York. Where do I live? -> You live in New York.
I'm 25 years old. How old am I? -> You are 25 years old.
My favorite color is blue. What's my favorite color? -> Your favorite color is blue.

# Multi-turn Context
I have a dog named Max. What's my dog's name? -> Your dog's name is Max.
I told you about my dog Max. He's 3 years old. How old is Max? -> Max is 3 years old.
Max loves to play fetch. What does Max love? -> Max loves to play fetch.

# Conversation Continuity
I'm planning a trip to Paris. Where am I going? -> You're going to Paris.
For my Paris trip, I need to book a hotel. What do I need to book? -> You need to book a hotel for your Paris trip.
I'm excited about visiting the Eiffel Tower in Paris. What am I excited about? -> You're excited about visiting the Eiffel Tower in Paris.

# Pronoun Resolution with Context
Sarah is my sister. She's a doctor. Who is a doctor? -> Sarah, your sister, is a doctor.
I bought a car. It's red. What color is the car? -> The car you bought is red.
Tom and I went hiking. He got tired. Who got tired? -> Tom got tired during the hiking trip.

# Topic Tracking
Let's talk about climate change. It's a serious issue. What are we discussing? -> We're discussing climate change.
Climate change causes rising temperatures. What does climate change cause? -> Climate change causes rising temperatures.
We should take action on climate change. What should we do? -> We should take action on climate change.

# Information Accumulation
I work at Google. Where do I work? -> You work at Google.
I'm a software engineer at Google. What's my job? -> You're a software engineer at Google.
As a software engineer at Google, I work on AI. What do you work on? -> You work on AI as a software engineer at Google.

# Temporal Context
Yesterday I went shopping. When did I go shopping? -> You went shopping yesterday.
Today I'm working from home. What are you doing today? -> You're working from home today.
Tomorrow I have a meeting. What do you have tomorrow? -> You have a meeting tomorrow.

# Preference Tracking
I love Italian food. What food do I love? -> You love Italian food.
I don't like spicy food. What don't you like? -> You don't like spicy food.
My favorite Italian dish is pasta. What's your favorite Italian dish? -> Your favorite Italian dish is pasta.

# Relationship Context
My mom is a teacher. What does your mom do? -> Your mom is a teacher.
My dad works with my mom at the same school. Where does your dad work? -> Your dad works at the same school as your mom.
Both my parents are teachers. What do your parents do? -> Both your parents are teachers.

# Goal Tracking
I want to learn Spanish. What do you want to learn? -> You want to learn Spanish.
I'm learning Spanish to travel to Spain. Why are you learning Spanish? -> You're learning Spanish to travel to Spain.
For my Spain trip, I'm studying Spanish. What are you studying for? -> You're studying Spanish for your Spain trip.

# Problem-Solution Context
I have a headache. What's wrong? -> You have a headache.
For my headache, I took medicine. What did you take? -> You took medicine for your headache.
The medicine helped my headache. Did the medicine help? -> Yes, the medicine helped your headache.

# Story Context
Once there was a princess. Who was there? -> There was a princess.
The princess lived in a castle. Where did the princess live? -> The princess lived in a castle.
In her castle, the princess had a dragon friend. What did the princess have? -> The princess had a dragon friend in her castle.

# Instruction Context
First, preheat the oven. What's the first step? -> The first step is to preheat the oven.
After preheating, mix the ingredients. What's next? -> After preheating, mix the ingredients.
Then bake the mixture. What do you do with the mixture? -> You bake the mixture.

# Opinion Context
I think the movie was great. What's your opinion of the movie? -> You think the movie was great.
The acting was excellent. What was excellent? -> The acting in the movie was excellent.
I'd recommend this movie. Would you recommend it? -> Yes, you'd recommend the movie.

# Comparison Context
I prefer coffee over tea. What do you prefer? -> You prefer coffee over tea.
Coffee has more caffeine than tea. What has more caffeine? -> Coffee has more caffeine than tea.
That's why I choose coffee. Why do you choose coffee? -> You choose coffee because it has more caffeine.

# Cause-Effect Context
I studied hard. What did you do? -> You studied hard.
Because I studied hard, I passed the exam. Why did you pass? -> You passed because you studied hard.
Passing the exam made me happy. What made you happy? -> Passing the exam made you happy.

# Location Context
I'm at the library. Where are you? -> You're at the library.
The library is quiet. How is the library? -> The library is quiet.
I'm studying at the quiet library. What are you doing? -> You're studying at the quiet library.

# Activity Context
I'm cooking dinner. What are you doing? -> You're cooking dinner.
I'm making pasta for dinner. What are you making? -> You're making pasta for dinner.
The pasta smells delicious. How does it smell? -> The pasta smells delicious.

# Emotional Context
I'm feeling happy today. How are you feeling? -> You're feeling happy today.
I'm happy because it's my birthday. Why are you happy? -> You're happy because it's your birthday.
My birthday party is tonight. When is your party? -> Your birthday party is tonight.

# Plan Context
I'm planning to exercise tomorrow. What are your plans? -> You're planning to exercise tomorrow.
I'll go to the gym tomorrow morning. When will you go? -> You'll go to the gym tomorrow morning.
At the gym, I'll do cardio. What will you do? -> You'll do cardio at the gym.

# Multiple Entity Tracking
Alice and Bob are my friends. Who are your friends? -> Alice and Bob are your friends.
Alice is a doctor and Bob is a teacher. What do they do? -> Alice is a doctor and Bob is a teacher.
I met Alice and Bob at university. Where did you meet them? -> You met Alice and Bob at university.

# Nested Context
I have a friend named Sarah. She has a dog named Max. Max is 5 years old. How old is Sarah's dog? -> Sarah's dog Max is 5 years old.
Sarah's dog Max loves treats. What does Max love? -> Max, Sarah's dog, loves treats.

# Conditional Context
If it rains, I'll stay home. What will you do if it rains? -> You'll stay home if it rains.
It's raining now. What are you doing? -> You're staying home because it's raining.

# Contrast Context
I like summer but not winter. What seasons do you like? -> You like summer but not winter.
Summer is warm while winter is cold. What's the difference? -> Summer is warm while winter is cold.
I prefer warm weather. What do you prefer? -> You prefer warm weather.

# Sequential Context
First I wake up, then I brush my teeth, then I eat breakfast. What's your routine? -> You wake up, brush your teeth, then eat breakfast.
After breakfast, I go to work. What do you do after breakfast? -> You go to work after breakfast.

# Hypothetical Context
If I won the lottery, I'd travel the world. What would you do? -> You'd travel the world if you won the lottery.
I'd visit Japan first. Where would you visit first? -> You'd visit Japan first.

# Correction Context
I said I'm 25, but actually I'm 26. How old are you? -> You're 26 years old (you corrected from 25).
I meant to say I live in Boston, not New York. Where do you live? -> You live in Boston (you corrected from New York).

# Clarification Context
I work in tech, specifically in AI. What field do you work in? -> You work in tech, specifically in AI.
By AI, I mean machine learning. What do you mean by AI? -> You mean machine learning when you say AI.

# Reference Context
I read a book yesterday. It was amazing. What was amazing? -> The book you read yesterday was amazing.
The book was about space. What was the book about? -> The book was about space.

# Implicit Context
I'm going to the store. I need milk. Why are you going? -> You're going to the store to get milk.
I'll be back in 30 minutes. When will you be back? -> You'll be back in 30 minutes from the store.
