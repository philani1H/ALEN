# Complex Questions
Why is the sky blue -> The sky appears blue because molecules in Earth's atmosphere scatter shorter wavelengths of light (blue) more than longer wavelengths (red). This is called Rayleigh scattering.
How does gravity work -> Gravity is the force of attraction between objects with mass. Einstein described it as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy.
What is consciousness -> Consciousness is awareness of one's own existence, thoughts, and surroundings. It's still not fully understood by science and remains a deep philosophical question.
Explain quantum mechanics -> Quantum mechanics describes the behavior of matter and energy at atomic and subatomic scales, where particles exhibit wave-particle duality and probabilistic behavior.

# Philosophy and Ethics
Is it ever okay to lie -> This is a complex ethical question. Most philosophers distinguish between harmful lies and white lies, considering context, intent, and consequences.
What is justice -> Justice involves fairness, equality, and appropriate consequences. Different theories emphasize different aspects: retribution, restoration, or distribution.
Do we have free will -> This is debated by philosophers and scientists. Some argue we have free will, others say we're determined by prior causes, and some propose compatibilism.
What makes something right or wrong -> Ethical frameworks differ: some focus on consequences (utilitarianism), others on duties (deontology), and some on virtues (virtue ethics).

# Science and Discovery
How was the universe created -> The Big Bang theory suggests the universe began from an extremely hot, dense state about 13.8 billion years ago and has been expanding ever since.
What is DNA -> DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is the molecule that carries genetic instructions for life. It's structured as a double helix and contains the code for making proteins.
How do vaccines work -> Vaccines train your immune system to recognize and fight specific pathogens by exposing it to a harmless version or part of the pathogen.
What is climate change -> Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns, primarily driven by human activities that increase greenhouse gases.
