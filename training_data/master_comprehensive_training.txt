# Mathematics
Q: What is 2 + 2?
A: 4

Q: What is 15 * 7?
A: 105

Q: What is the square root of 144?
A: 12

Q: Solve for x: 2x + 5 = 15
A: x = 5. Subtract 5 from both sides: 2x = 10, then divide by 2: x = 5.

Q: What is the derivative of x²?
A: 2x. Using the power rule: d/dx(x^n) = nx^(n-1), so d/dx(x²) = 2x^(2-1) = 2x.

Q: What is the integral of 2x?
A: x² + C, where C is the constant of integration.

Q: What is the Pythagorean theorem?
A: In a right triangle, a² + b² = c², where c is the hypotenuse and a and b are the other two sides.

Q: What is 25% of 80?
A: 20. Calculate: 0.25 × 80 = 20.

Q: What is the area of a circle with radius 5?
A: Approximately 78.54 square units. Using A = πr², A = π(5)² = 25π ≈ 78.54.

Q: What is a prime number?
A: A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that has no positive divisors other than 1 and itself. Examples: 2, 3, 5, 7, 11.

# Science
Q: What is photosynthesis?
A: Photosynthesis is the process by which plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce oxygen and energy in the form of sugar.

Q: What is gravity?
A: Gravity is a fundamental force that attracts objects with mass toward each other. On Earth, it gives weight to physical objects and causes them to fall toward the ground.

Q: What is DNA?
A: DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) is a molecule that carries genetic instructions for the development, functioning, growth, and reproduction of all known organisms.

Q: What is the speed of light?
A: The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 299,792,458 meters per second (about 186,282 miles per second).

Q: What causes seasons?
A: Seasons are caused by Earth's tilted axis as it orbits the sun. Different parts of Earth receive varying amounts of sunlight throughout the year.

Q: What is an atom?
A: An atom is the smallest unit of matter that retains the properties of an element. It consists of a nucleus (protons and neutrons) surrounded by electrons.

Q: What is evolution?
A: Evolution is the process by which species change over time through natural selection, genetic variation, and adaptation to their environment.

Q: What is the water cycle?
A: The water cycle is the continuous movement of water on, above, and below Earth's surface through evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection.

# Programming
Q: What is a variable in programming?
A: A variable is a named storage location in memory that holds a value which can be changed during program execution.

Q: What is a function?
A: A function is a reusable block of code that performs a specific task. It can take inputs (parameters) and return outputs.

Q: What is a loop?
A: A loop is a programming construct that repeats a block of code multiple times until a condition is met.

Q: What is an array?
A: An array is a data structure that stores a collection of elements, typically of the same type, in contiguous memory locations.

Q: What is recursion?
A: Recursion is a programming technique where a function calls itself to solve a problem by breaking it down into smaller, similar subproblems.

Q: What is object-oriented programming?
A: Object-oriented programming (OOP) is a programming paradigm based on objects that contain data (attributes) and code (methods). Key concepts include encapsulation, inheritance, and polymorphism.

Q: What is a bug in programming?
A: A bug is an error, flaw, or fault in a computer program that causes it to produce incorrect or unexpected results.

Q: What is debugging?
A: Debugging is the process of finding and fixing bugs or errors in computer programs.

Q: What is inductive reasoning?
A: Inductive reasoning is a logical process where multiple premises, all believed true, are combined to obtain a specific conclusion. It moves from specific observations to broader generalizations.
